Quick Start Guide
← Home
FieldIQ helps field teams capture every customer interaction in seconds, automatically creating structured visit reports, follow-up actions, and management insights.
⚡ Your First 2 Minutes
1. Open the FieldIQ link → select your name → enter your PIN
2. Tap Log Visit
3. Search for the account, then pick a date, territory, and type
4. Tap 🎙️ and just talk about what happened — tap again to stop
5. Tap Process Visit Log
✅ That's it. FieldIQ writes the report, saves it, and creates any follow-up tasks for you.
1Getting In
Open the FieldIQ link on your phone or laptop.
The first time on a new device, you may be asked to confirm your email — this just checks you're an approved user before letting you in.
Then select your name from the list and enter your PIN.
You're in. To switch to a different name later, tap Switch User on the home screen.
⚠️ Logged in as the wrong person? Tap Switch User, pick the right name, and enter your PIN.
2Logging a Visit
1. Tap Log Visit.
2. Search and pick the account.
3. Set the date, territory, and type — in person, phone, or virtual.
4. Tap 🎙️ and talk naturally about what happened. Tap it again when you're done.
5. Your words appear as text — quickly check it looks right, fix anything obvious.
6. Tap Process Visit Log. FieldIQ takes it from there.
✅ You don't need to say who you are — FieldIQ already knows from your login. Just talk about the visit itself.
3What Should I Actually Say?
There's no script. Just talk like you're telling a colleague what happened.
30–60 seconds is usually enough. The more specific you are, the better your report.
Who did you speak to? Names and roles help a lot.
What was it about? A sales conversation, a training session, a technical question, a service issue — whatever it actually was.
What did they ask for, or say? A quotation, a price, samples, training, a technical answer, a complaint — say the specific thing.
What did you agree to do next? Be specific — "send the quotation by Thursday" works better than "follow up."
❌ Too vague: "Good meeting, will follow up next week."
✅ Better: "Met Sarah, the Operations Manager. She asked for a quotation and the technical datasheet. Agreed I'd send both by Thursday and she'll review with her team next Tuesday."
FieldIQ can only report what you actually say — it doesn't guess or assume. The more concrete the detail, the more useful the report.
4Adding a New Account
Can't find the account in the list? Tick ☑ Account not listed.
Enter the account name and city.
Carry on as normal — record your voice note and submit.
It's saved with a New Account flag so your manager can confirm it.
5After You Submit — What You Get
FieldIQ turns your recording into a structured report in seconds, with:
→ Visit Summary
→ Key Contacts Met
→ Outcomes & Decisions
→ Next Steps
→ Opportunity Signals
→ Sentiment
→ Priority Level
→ A suggested follow-up date, if you mentioned one
Everything saves automatically — no paperwork, no separate form.
6Priority Levels — What They Mean
FieldIQ sets a priority on every visit based on what was actually said.
High — Something concrete was asked for (a quotation, a contract, training, a technical evaluation), someone committed to a next step, or there's a risk or complaint that needs attention.
Medium — A next step is needed, the conversation went well, or the relationship is developing.
Low — Just informational, nothing concrete on the table yet.
7Your Calendar & Updating Plans
Your Calendar shows upcoming follow-ups created from your visits.
Plans change — a meeting gets moved, becomes a phone call, or the agenda shifts.
Open the day on your Calendar and tap 🎙️ Log Update next to the item.
Speak naturally about what changed — same as logging any visit. No new screen to learn.
If you mention a new date, FieldIQ will suggest it — you confirm the actual reschedule yourself on the Calendar.
✅ You never have to decide "is this a follow-up or a new visit?" Just speak — FieldIQ handles the rest.
8Visit History
Tap Visit History to see everything you've logged, newest first.
Each card shows the account, date, type, priority, and summary.
New accounts are marked with a New Account badge.
ℹ️ This is a record, not an editor. Spot a mistake? Ask your manager to correct it.
9One Identity, Everywhere
You pick your name once, at login. Every screen after that already knows it's you.
This is what keeps your visits, calendar, and history correctly grouped under your name.
⚠️ Logged in as someone else by mistake? Use Switch User before logging anything.
For Managers
MANAGEROperations — Your Morning Check-In
Operations is the first place to look every day. It has two views:
Today — what's been logged today, who's been active, and anything that needs a closer look.
Territory Board — one card per rep, showing how recently they've been in the field and whether anything needs attention.
Rep Activity shows you the moment a rep logs an update on something you're tracking — tap it to see what changed.
Attention Required collects the things that genuinely need a look: reps who've gone quiet, overdue actions, accounts that haven't been visited in a while.
✅ If you only check one screen a day, make it this one.
MANAGERManager Calendar
See every rep's upcoming follow-ups in one shared calendar.
Filter to a single rep if you only want to see their schedule.
This is a view, not an editor — only the rep can reschedule or cancel their own items. You'll always know what's planned without needing to touch anyone's calendar.
MANAGEROpen Actions
A live list of everything your team has committed to — quotations to send, samples to deliver, follow-ups due.
Filter by rep, or by status — open, overdue, due today, completed.
Send a reminder to a rep directly from here if something's overdue.
MANAGERAccount Directory
Every account across the whole team, grouped by territory, with full visit history at a glance.
Open any account to see who's visited, what was discussed, and what's outstanding — useful if a rep is away or an account changes hands.
10Install FieldIQ as an App
FieldIQ can be added to your phone or computer so it opens like a normal app — no need to find a browser tab each time.
Windows: click the install icon in the address bar.
Android: open the ⋮ menu in Chrome and tap Install app.
iPhone: open the link in Safari, tap Share, then Add to Home Screen.
ℹ️ See the separate FieldIQ Installation Guide for full step-by-step instructions and troubleshooting.
